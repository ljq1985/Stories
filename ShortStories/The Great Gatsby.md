# The Great Gatsby
## Chapter 1
- My name is Nick Carraway, and I lived in a small house in West Egg. Long Island. Right next to my house, there was an enormous mansion where a man named Jay Gatsby lived. He was a rich and mysterious person who often hosted big parties at his place.
- One night, before I even met him, I saw him standing on his lawn, He was reaching toward something that turned out to be a green light at the end if a dock. How strange.
## Chapter 2
- One day, I received an invitation from Gatsby to attend one of his parties. I was surprised because not many people got personal invitations from him. At the party, I met Gatsby for the first time. He was a friendly and charming man.
- He told me that he knew my cousin Daisy Buchanan who lived across the bay in East Egg with her husband Tom. Gatsby confessed to me that he loved Daisy. He revealed that they were in love before he left to fight in World War Ⅰ,Sadly, when he returned, Daisy was already married to Tom. Gatsby had bought this house in West Egg just to be close to her. He had thrown all these extravagant parties in the hope that one day Daisy might attend.
## Chapter 3
- He asked for my help to arrange a meeting with her. I agreed and invited Daisy over for tea without telling her that Gatsby would be there too.
- When they saw each other, it was clear that the old feelings were still there. "If it wasn't for the mist we could see your home across the bay," Gatsby said to her. "You have a green light that burns all night at the end of your dock."
- This made me realize what or, more specifically, whom Gatsby was yearning for that night I saw him on his lawn.
## Chapter 4
- They started seeing each other again. Daisy was moved by Gatsby's love for her, but she was also scared of leaving her comfortable life with Tom.
- Meanwhile, Tom became suspicious of Daisy's frequent absences. One day, he decided to confront Gatsby and Daisy. In a heated argument, Gatsby insisted that Daisy had always loved him and never loved Tom.
- Daisy could not confirm this. She admitted that she loved them both. 
- After the confrontation, Gatsby and Daisy drove home together in Gatsby's car. Daisy was driving when she hit and killed a woman named Myrtle, who happened to be Tom's lover. They left the accident scene and returned to Gatsby's house.
- Gatsby took the blame for the accident, which proved his deep love for Daisy. Unfortunately, Myrtle's husband George believed that Gatsby was the killer because it was his car. In his grief and anger, he shot and killed Gatsby in his swimming pool, then killed himself.
## Chapter 5
- After Gatsby's death, I was one of the few people who attended his funeral. Daisy and Tom moved away, leaving no forwarding address. I decided to return to my hometown in the Midwest, definitely disappointed with the lifestyle I had experienced in West Egg.
- I realize now that Gatsby's dream of a life with Daisy was just an illusion. He was a man who believed in the green light, a future that, year by year, he strove and hoped for. Had he not succeeded that day? It did not matter. The next day, he ran faster, stretched his arms out farther.
