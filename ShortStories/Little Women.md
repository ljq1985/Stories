# Little Women
## Chapter 1
- One upon a time, there were four sisters called Jo, Meg, Beth, and Amy March. They lived in a small town with their kind mother, Marmee. Their father was away because of the war. The family did not have much money, but they had a lot of love.
- The sisters loved to play and learn together. Jo liked to write stories. She wanted to be a famous writer. Meg loved dresses and wanted to wear pretty things. Beth was quiet and loved to play the piano. The youngest sister, Amy, liked to paint and hoped to become an artist.
- They sometimes felt sad about not having much money. But their mother taught them to be kind and generous. Marmee said that love was better than things.
## Chapter 2
- They liked to visit their Aunt March. She was rich and lived in a big house. The sister saw many beautiful things there. Meg looked at the pretty dressed and wished she had some too.
- One day, Jo met a kind old man called Mr.Laurence. He was their neighbor. Everyone liked him. especially Beth. She found that he loved music too. He and his young nephew Laurie became friends with the March family.
## Chapter 3
- One evening, Marmee had to leave because their father was ill. The sisters were worried, but they knew they had to be strong. While Marmee was away, they took care for each other.
- With their mother gone, the girls understood that being togeter was very important. They helped each other with their dreams. Jo wrote a funny paly for them to act together. They used old clothes to make costumes.
- Meg learned that pretty dresses do not make a person happy. Beth played sweet music that made them feel better. Amy's paintings made the house beauiful.
## Chapter 4
- When Marmee come home, the sisters were very happy. They realized that being together was very important. They danced and laughed together. The four sisters and their mother had a special bond. Each sister grew up.
- Jo got married but continued writing because it was her passion. Being strong and independent helped her have a good marriage. Meg continued to appreciate the simple joys of life. She never stopped caring for her family and house. Beth kept her love of music. She continued to use her talent to make other people happy, in her own home and in others.
- Amy got married like her older sister Jo. But, she never stopped her art either. Her art became even better. Like Jo, she had hard times in her marriage. Yet, she had a life as beautiful as her pictures.
- Each of them understood that their love made them strong. They were rich with love, just like their mother taught them. They were the sweet March sisters, with a story of love and dreams.
